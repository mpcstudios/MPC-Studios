We built a compact, practical site for the [Port Mansfield Police Department](https://www.portmansfieldpd.gov/) using [Lovable](https://lovable.dev/)  as the foundation for Website Development, and [Vercel](https://vercel.com/) for hosting and deployment. This post walks through the challenges in developing a website using AI, building the features that matter to a small coastal community, and the QA work that turned a fast AI-assisted build into a production-ready municipal site.

## Project snapshot

The budget was tight, the timeline was short, and the goal was straightforward: give residents, property owners, and visitors fast, reliable access to police information on any device. We used Lovable to generate the base website, applied targeted human edits, modifications, and enhancements, and deployed on Vercel for secure, speedy delivery to the user.

## Homepage: clear mission and quick paths

The homepage sets the tone with a community-first mission and direct links to the most-used items: contact info, services, and forms. No marketing fluff — just immediate clarity so visitors can act fast.

## QA lessons: the difference between fast and finished

AI accelerates page generation, but proper production sites need human QA. We addressed four key areas:

-   Sitemaps — validated a clean sitemap.xml so search engines can discover pages reliably.
-   SEO data — audited titles, meta descriptions, open graph, canonical URLs, and schema for consistency.
-   Deep linking — configured routes and fallbacks so subpages resolve predictably and are shareable.
-   Scroll behavior — implemented scroll reset on route change so pages always load from the top and anchors work.

![QA checklist highlighting sitemaps, SEO, routing, and scroll behavior](https://mpcstudios-media.s3.us-east-1.amazonaws.com/mpc-studios/blog/port-mansfield-pd-lovable-vercel/538ac8f9-7d14-4b86-b5cf-5174ec0aec77.png)

### What is Lovable for Website Development and why use it for websites?

Lovable Website Development is an AI generator that can be used for initial site structure and styling. It speeds up builds, letting teams focus time on content, accessibility, and QA — which is ideal for smaller budgets and tight timelines.

### How does Vercel fit into the workflow?

Vercel handles deployments, SSL, and performance optimizations. Once the website is finished, it has to be “live” somewhere for users to access.  Vercel makes rollouts simple and keeps sites fast and secure with minimal ongoing ops work.

### What are the non-negotiable QA steps after an AI-assisted build?

Validate sitemap.xml, audit SEO metadata and social previews, ensure deep linking works with proper routing and fallbacks, and fix scroll/anchor behavior so each page loads predictably — these are the checks that separate “it loads” from “it’s built right.”
